\[
k_{t+1}=(1-\delta )k_{t}+\phi (x_{t}/k_{t})k_{t}
\]
The elasticity of the price of capital wrt $x/k$ is
\[
\eta (\frac{x}{k})=-\frac{\phi ^{\prime \prime }}{\phi ^{\prime }}\frac{x}{k}
\]
In our notation
\[
k_{t+1}=(1-\delta )k_{t}+\left[ \frac{x_{t}}{k_{t}}-g(\frac{x_{t}}{k_{t}})%
\right] k_{t}
\]
So
\[
\phi (x_{t}/k_{t})=\frac{x_{t}}{k_{t}}-g(\frac{x_{t}}{k_{t}})
\]
\[
\phi ^{\prime }=1-g^{\prime }\text{ and }\phi ^{\prime \prime }=-g^{\prime
\prime }
\]
At a steady state, let $x/k=b$ where $b=$ $\delta +g$ then the steady state
elasticity is  
\[
\eta =-\frac{\phi ^{\prime \prime }(b)}{\phi ^{\prime }(b)}b=\frac{g^{\prime
\prime }(b)}{1-g^{\prime }(b)}b
\]
Our functional form is 
\[
g(\frac{x}{k})=\frac{a}{2}\left( \frac{x}{k}-b\right) ^{2}
\]
so 
\[
g^{\prime }(\frac{x}{k})=a\left( \frac{x}{k}-b\right) \text{ and }g^{\prime
\prime }(\frac{x}{k})=a
\]
Hence
\[
\eta =ab
\]
Bernanke, Gertler, Gilchrist use $\eta =.25,$ Baxter and Crucini (IER) use $%
\eta =\frac{1}{15}=.07,$ King and Wolman use $\eta =1/2$ (to see this look
at table 1. p 40 in the King Wolman, Inflation targeting in a st. louis
model of the 21 century, NBER wp 5507). 

We suggest $\eta =.25$
